Anthony Michael Bourdain

The Rockstar Chef and World Traveler

1956-2018

Anthony Bourdain, seated right, drinks with former U.S. president Barack Obama in a restaurant in Hanoi, Vietnam.

    Here's a timeline of Anthony Bourdain's life:

  • 1956 - Born in New York City, U.S.
  • 1975 - Drops out of Vassar College after two years to pursue cooking as a career
  • 1978 - Graduates from The Culinary Institute of America
  • 1998 - Becomes executive chef at Brasserie Les Halles
  • 1999 - Publishes in the New Yorker the article Don't Eat Before Reading This, which is about the behind-the-scenes look at restaurant-kitchens.
  • 2000 - Expands on his New Yorker article by writing a book called Kitchen Confidential: Adventures in the Culinary Underbelly, which garners rave reviews and becomes a New York Times bestseller
  • 2002 - Hosts the Food Network food and travel show A Cook's Tour until 2003.
  • 2005 - Starts hosting the Travel Channel show Anthony Bourdain: No Reservations, which lasts for seven years with 142 episodes and is nominated for an Emmy Award for Outstanding Informational Programming .
  • 2006 - During a shoot in Beirut for No Reservations, Bourdain and his crew get caught in the Israel-Lebannon conflict. They escape with the help of the U.S. Marine Corps.
  • 2011 - Hosts the short-lived show The Layover which explores cities within 24 to 48 hours.
  • 2013 - Anthony Bourdain: Parts Unknown premiered on CNN, a show that focuses on foreign cuisines, cultures and politics. The show wins a Peabody Award and lasts five years with 96 episodes.
  • 2014 - Bourdain dines with celebrated chef and a personal hero of his, Paul Bocuse, in Lyons, France.
  • 2016 - Bourdain dines with then U.S. president Barack Obama over bun cha, a noodle pork dish, in Hanoi, Vietnam.
  • 2017 - Conferred the honorary degree of Doctor of Humane Letters honoris causa in the Culinary Arts by The Culinary Institute of America.
  • 2018 - Bourdain commits suicide by hanging in Kayserberg, France, during a film shoot for an episode of Parts Unknown.

"He taught us about food - but more importantly, about its ability to bring us together. To make us a little less afraid of the unknown. We'll miss him."
--former U.S. President Barack Obama
